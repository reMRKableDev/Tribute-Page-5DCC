Julius K. Nyerere

1922

Born in Butiama, Tanganyika.

1949

First Tanganyikan accepted to study at a British University (Edinburgh).

1952

Graduated from Edinburgh University.

1953

Elected president of Tanganyika African Association (TAA).

1954

Helped transform the TAA to a new political party, Tanganyika African National Union (TANU).

1955

Travels as a TANU representative to the United Nations trusteeship council in New York City to petition a target date for the independence of Tanganyika.

1958-59

Together with TANU, won a large number of seats on the Tanganyikan Legislative Council.

1960

Calls for the formation of an East African Federation at conference in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.

1961

Elected first Prime Minister upon Tanganyika's independence. Resigns a month later to devote time to writing and synthesizing his views of government and African unity.

1962

Elected President to new Tanganyikan Republic. Published, "Ujamaaâ€”The Basis for African Socialism".

1964

Became President of United Republic of Tanzania (a union between Tanganyika and Zanzibar).

1967

Presented the Arusha Declaration at TANU National Executive meeting. The declaration affirmed the government's commitment to building a democratic socialist state and stressed the development of an ethos of self-reliance.

1975

Reelected as President of Tanzania.

1979

Sent Tanzanian army to assist in the overthrowing of Idi Amin in Uganda.

1985

Resigns as President of Tanzania.

1990

Resigns as Chairman of CCM.

1990-99

Remained politically active on international affairs.

1999

Died at age 77.
